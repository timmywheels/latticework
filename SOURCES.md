# Sources & attribution

The material behind Latticework. Munger is the **seed** of the catalog, not its boundary: these are the primary texts his models were mined from, plus the secondary compilations audited for provenance. Structured form: [`data/sources.json`](data/sources.json) — and every model links to its own sources on its plate.

Every URL was fetched during research and verified against the research record — none reconstructed from memory. Per-model source links live on each entry in `data/latticework-catalog.json` (`sources`).


## Primary — Munger talks

- **Poor Charlie's Almanack, Talk One: Harvard School Commencement Speech** (1986) — Charlie Munger. Stripe Press
  https://www.stripe.press/poor-charlies-almanack/talk-one
  *Cited for:* Poor Charlie's Almanack opening talk; primary chapter of the canon, cited for Munger's inversion heuristic applied to life advice.
  *Note:* Talk One of Poor Charlie's Almanack (Stripe Press digital edition), edited by Peter D. Kaufman — Munger's Harvard School (Los Angeles) commencement address, 1986, the well-known inversion talk on how to guarantee a life of misery.

- **A Lesson on Elementary, Worldly Wisdom** (1994) — Charlie Munger. Farnam Street
  https://fs.blog/great-talks/a-lesson-on-worldly-wisdom/
  *Cited for:* Primary source for the '80 or 90 important models will carry 90% of the freight' figure, the origin of the 'latticework' metaphor, and Munger-named math/business/microeconomics models (compound interest, permutations & combinations, Fermat/Pascal, decision trees, competitive destruction, surfing, circle of competence, pari-mutuel markets).
  *Note:* USC Business School, 1994. Originally published as a transcript in Outstanding Investor Digest (May 5, 1995) and reprinted in the valueplays 'Best of Charlie Munger' PDF; Farnam Street hosts this full-text copy.

- **The Psychology of Human Misjudgment — 1995 Harvard speech ('24 Standard Causes of Human Misjudgment')** (1995) — Charlie Munger. Buffett Munger Wisdom (WordPress archive)
  https://buffettmungerwisdom.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/mungerspeech_june_95.pdf
  *Cited for:* The 1995 speech; cited for the '24 Standard Causes' framing, item-one incentives (reinforcement/incentives, FedEx night-shift example), the coined 'man-with-a-hammer syndrome,' and the lollapalooza-effect Q&A.
  *Note:* June 1995 Harvard speech, presented as the '24 Standard Causes of Human Misjudgment'; the Whitney Tilson transcription actually enumerates only 22 items with bracketed corrections. Distinct edition from the 2005 rewrite (separate entry). Alternate full transcript at singjupost.com.

- **Poor Charlie's Almanack, Talk Three: A Lesson on Elementary, Worldly Wisdom, Revisited** (1996) — Charlie Munger. Stripe Press
  https://www.stripe.press/poor-charlies-almanack/talk-three
  *Cited for:* Book edition of the 1996 Stanford talk; cited for restating the latticework tied back to USC, the ~100-model target, and a large set of Munger-named psychology models plus the 'use them as a checklist' method.
  *Note:* Talk Three of Poor Charlie's Almanack (Stripe Press digital edition), edited by Peter D. Kaufman — the canonical book edition of Munger's Stanford Law School 1996 talk. Reproduces the material first published as the Outstanding Investor Digest Dec 1997 transcript (separate entry).

- **Poor Charlie’s Almanack, Talk Four: Practical Thought About Practical Thought?** (1996) — Charlie Munger. Stripe Press
  https://www.stripe.press/poor-charlies-almanack/talk-four
  *Cited for:* The turning-$2M-into-$2T Coca-Cola thought experiment; Pavlovian/operant conditioning, autocatalysis, social proof, the five helpful notions.
  *Note:* Poor Charlie’s Almanack (Stripe Press digital edition), edited by Peter D. Kaufman.

- **Worldly Wisdom Revisited (Stanford Law School, 1996) — Outstanding Investor Digest transcript** (1996) — Charlie Munger. Outstanding Investor Digest
  https://mjbaldbard.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/oid-dec-1997-cm-worldly-wisdom-revisited.pdf
  *Cited for:* First-generation transcript of the 1996 talk; cited for the 'best 100 or so models' target and its discipline list, lollapalooza effects, critical mass (physics) and autocatalysis (E.O. Wilson), Jacobi's 'invert, always invert,' and the physiology/Huxley biology models.
  *Note:* Stanford Law School, April 19, 1996. First-generation transcript published in Outstanding Investor Digest, Dec 29, 1997 (pp. 24-31); scanned PDF archived at mjbaldbard.wordpress.com. Same talk later reproduced as Talk Three of Poor Charlie's Almanack (separate entry).

- **Poor Charlie’s Almanack, Talk Five: The Need for More Multidisciplinary Skills from Professionals** (1998) — Charlie Munger. Stripe Press
  https://www.stripe.press/poor-charlies-almanack/talk-five
  *Cited for:* Harvard Law School 50th reunion, 1998; multidisciplinary worldly wisdom, man-with-a-hammer, the four-discipline hard-science ethos, tragedy of the commons, invisible hand/foot.
  *Note:* Poor Charlie’s Almanack (Stripe Press digital edition), edited by Peter D. Kaufman.

- **Poor Charlie’s Almanack, Talk Six: Investment Practices of Leading Charitable Foundations** (1998) — Charlie Munger. Stripe Press
  https://www.stripe.press/poor-charlies-almanack/talk-six
  *Cited for:* Croupier’s take / frictional costs, overconfidence, indexing, buried covariance, focus investing, self-insurance.
  *Note:* Poor Charlie’s Almanack (Stripe Press digital edition), edited by Peter D. Kaufman.

- **Poor Charlie’s Almanack, Talk Eight: The Great Financial Scandal of 2003** (2000) — Charlie Munger. Stripe Press
  https://www.stripe.press/poor-charlies-almanack/talk-eight
  *Cited for:* Fictional Quant Tech accounting parable; functional-equivalence test, dollop-by-dollop gradualism, Ponzi/chain-letter mechanics, reputational rub-off.
  *Note:* Poor Charlie’s Almanack (Stripe Press digital edition), edited by Peter D. Kaufman.

- **Poor Charlie’s Almanack, Talk Seven: Breakfast Meeting of the Philanthropy Roundtable** (2000) — Charlie Munger. Stripe Press
  https://www.stripe.press/poor-charlies-almanack/talk-seven
  *Cited for:* Galbraith’s bezzle and Munger’s febezzle/foolexures extension, wealth effect, Keynesian multiplier, crowd folly.
  *Note:* Poor Charlie’s Almanack (Stripe Press digital edition), edited by Peter D. Kaufman.

- **Poor Charlie’s Almanack, Talk Nine: Academic Economics — Strengths and Faults** (2003) — Charlie Munger. Stripe Press
  https://www.stripe.press/poor-charlies-almanack/talk-nine
  *Cited for:* UCSB Herb Kay Memorial Lecture, 2003; the nine faults — fatal unconnectedness, physics envy, febezzlement, comparative advantage & second-order effects, Gresham’s law extension, opportunity cost, multidisciplinarity.
  *Note:* Poor Charlie’s Almanack (Stripe Press digital edition), edited by Peter D. Kaufman.

- **Poor Charlie’s Almanack, Talk Ten: USC Gould School of Law Commencement** (2007) — Charlie Munger. Stripe Press
  https://www.stripe.press/poor-charlies-almanack/talk-ten
  *Cited for:* Learning machine, inversion, the iron prescription, seamless web of deserved trust, assiduity, Planck vs chauffeur knowledge, avoiding envy/resentment/self-pity.
  *Note:* Poor Charlie’s Almanack (Stripe Press digital edition), edited by Peter D. Kaufman.

## Secondary — compilation books

- **Investing: The Last Liberal Art (orig. 'Latticework: The New Investing')** (2000/2013) — Robert G. Hagstrom. Columbia Business School Publishing
  *Cited for:* Foundational book-length latticework elaboration; the source of many non-Munger discipline models later amplified by others.
  *Note:* First published as 'Latticework: The New Investing' (Texere, 2000); reissued as 'Investing: The Last Liberal Art' (2001); substantially revised 2nd ed., Columbia Business School Publishing, 2013 (adds a Mathematics chapter and Kahneman/Stanovich/Tetlock-updated Decision Making). Earliest book-length elaboration of Munger's latticework and a major channel through which non-Munger models (equilibrium, complex adaptive systems, pragmatism) entered the genre. Hagstrom does not claim the disciplines are Munger's: Ch.1 recounts Munger's 1994 USC talk, then presents his OWN liberal-arts/Great Books selection (St. John's College reading list reprinted as an appendix). Provenance audit: 54 items, 7 direct-Munger. No book-catalog URL in the vetted set — see the CFA Institute review entry for the linkable reference.

- **Seeking Wisdom: From Darwin to Munger** (2003/2007) — Peter Bevelin. Post Scriptum AB
  *Cited for:* 49-item misjudgment-and-thinking-tools compilation in the Munger/Darwin tradition.
  *Note:* Post Scriptum AB, 2003 (3rd ed. 2007). Structure: 28 psychological reasons for misjudgment, 9 physics/math causes, 12 thinking tools; appendix reproduces Munger's 1986 Harvard School commencement address plus Munger/Buffett quotes. Bevelin does not claim the lists are Munger's — preface: 'The ideas in it are built largely from the works and thoughts of others.' Part Two's 28 reasons reorganize and expand Munger's 24 'standard causes of human misjudgment' (~17 of 28 map directly to Munger). Provenance audit: 49 items, 27 direct-Munger. No canonical/citable URL for the book appears in the vetted source set, so url is intentionally empty.

- **Charlie Munger: The Complete Investor** (2015) — Tren Griffin. Columbia University Press
  https://cup.columbia.edu/book/charlie-munger/9780231170987/
  *Cited for:* Book-length Munger synthesis; its 25iq companion supplies the 165-item model list audited for this catalog.
  *Note:* The book deliberately omits a model list; the discipline-by-discipline ~100-model list ('A partial list of some Mental Models used by Charlie Munger, compiled from many sources') lives in Griffin's companion 25iq.com post 'A Dozen Things I've Learned from Charlie Munger about Mental Models and Worldly Wisdom' (Aug 22, 2015) — no verbatim URL for that post exists in the vetted source set. Griffin explicitly frames the compilation as his own: Munger 'has never prepared a complete list covering all disciplines.' Provenance audit (book + 25iq): 165 items, 63 direct-Munger.


## Secondary — web compilations

- **Charlie Munger Mental Models (Full 129 List + Latticework Guide)** — J.D. Meier. Sources of Insight
  https://sourcesofinsight.com/charlie-munger-mental-models/
  *Cited for:* 129-item Munger mental-model compilation with an accompanying latticework guide.
  *Note:* Title ('Full 129 List') and heading ('Charlie Munger's 129 Mental Models') imply canonicity, but the body hedges: 'Some are Munger's own labels; others are standard models he explicitly and repeatedly used or endorsed,' and its own Q&A concedes Munger said he used '80 or 90 pretty well.' Presented as Meier's Munger-inspired curation, not Munger's own list. Provenance audit: 129 items, 60 direct-Munger.

- **Farnam Street Mental Models (master list of ~100 models)** — Shane Parrish (Farnam Street). Farnam Street
  https://fs.blog/mental-models/
  *Cited for:* Largest secondary latticework taxonomy (~100 models across 8 disciplines); the main channel expanding Munger's mental-model canon.
  *Note:* Site named after Berkshire Hathaway's street address, with Munger cited as its inspiration. Presents 98 top-level models in 8 discipline buckets as Farnam Street's OWN curation ('The Big Ideas From The Big Disciplines'), fronted by a single Munger latticework epigraph — not offered as Munger's own list. Content mirrors Parrish's 'The Great Mental Models' book series. Provenance audit: 101 items checked, 26 direct-Munger, 24 plausible, 51 community additions. (Farnam Street's separate PHM transcript is published with Munger's written permission — that copy is cataloged in the primary group.)

  *Cited for:* Skeptical, practice-oriented critique of the mental-model compilation approach.
  *Note:* Companion to 'The Mental Model Fallacy'; argues for practice-based acquisition over list-memorization of Munger-style models. Skeptical / attribution-audit angle.

- **The Mental Model Fallacy** — Cedric Chin. Commoncog
  https://commoncog.com/the-mental-model-fallacy/
  *Cited for:* Skeptical counterweight on the value and provenance of mental-model lists.
